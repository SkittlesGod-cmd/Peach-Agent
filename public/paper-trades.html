How paper trading works

Paper trades are stored in SQLite alongside your real portfolio. Each trade has a direction (long or short), an entry price, an optional share count, and an optional thesis note. Peach fetches live prices at query time to compute your current P&L — nothing is simulated ahead of time.

Trades can be opened and closed from Discord or the CLI. Closed trades keep their entry and exit prices permanently, so you can review your track record at any time.

  1. 01

    Open a position

    Use the !trade command in Discord. Specify direction, ticker, entry price, optional share count, and an optional thesis note:

    # Long 5 shares of NVDA at $890, with a thesis:
!trade LONG NVDA 890 5 Jensen keynote catalyst

# Short TSLA at $240 with no share count:
!trade SHORT TSLA 240 overvalued on rate risk

# Peach confirms and assigns a trade ID:
# Trade #4 opened — LONG NVDA @ $890.00 (5 shares)

    The share count is optional — if omitted, Peach logs the position without a size so you can still track direction and P&L percentage without committing to a quantity.

  2. 02

    Check open trades

    Use !trades to see all open positions with live unrealised P&L fetched at query time:

    !trades

# Peach responds with live P&L for each open trade:
# #3  LONG  AAPL   entry $182  now $194.20  +$12.20 (+6.7%)
# #4  LONG  NVDA   entry $890  now $941.50  +$51.50 (+5.8%)  5 shares → +$257.50
# #5  SHORT TSLA   entry $240  now $228.10  +$11.90 (+5.0%)
  3. 03

    Close a trade

    Close any open trade by ID with an exit price. Peach records the P&L and marks it done:

    # Close trade #4 (NVDA long) at today's price:
!closetr 4 950

# Peach confirms with final P&L:
# Trade #4 closed — LONG NVDA $890 → $950  +$60.00 (+6.7%)  5 shares → +$300.00
  4. 04

    Review your track record

    Run !trades all to see both open and closed trades with full history:

    !trades all

# Open:
# #3  LONG  AAPL   entry $182  now $194.20  +6.7%  [open]
# #5  SHORT TSLA   entry $240  now $228.10  +5.0%  [open]
#
# Closed:
# #4  LONG  NVDA   entry $890  exit $950    +6.7%  +$300 total  [closed]
# #1  LONG  MSFT   entry $410  exit $438    +6.8%             [closed]
# #2  SHORT QQQ    entry $465  exit $451    +3.0%             [closed]
  5. 05

    Ask the agent about your trades New

    The agent can reason about your paper trades in natural language. Ask it to review your ideas before you open a position, or to summarise your performance:

    # Ask the agent to review a thesis before opening:
!peach I'm thinking of going long NVDA at 890, what do you think?

# Or review your open book:
!peach how are my paper trades doing relative to the broader market?

    The agent has access to get_quote, get_technicals, and get_portfolio so it can cross-reference your paper position against RSI, SMA levels, and recent news before giving an opinion.

Command reference

!trade LONG NVDA 890 5 thesis Open a long position in NVDA at $890, 5 shares. Thesis is optional and stored with the trade.
!trade SHORT TSLA 240 overvalued Open a short position in TSLA at $240 with a thesis note.
!trades List all open paper trades with live unrealised P&L.
!trades all List open and closed trades. Closed entries show entry, exit, and final P&L.
!closetr 4 950 Close trade #4 at $950. Peach records the final P&L and marks the trade done.

Storage and persistence

Paper trades are stored in the paper_trades table inside portfolio.db — the same SQLite database as your real positions and price alerts. They survive daemon restarts and are included in the daily backup if you've configured one.

FieldDescription
idAuto-incrementing trade ID, referenced in !closetr.
tickerThe ticker symbol (e.g. NVDA, TSLA).
directionLONG or SHORT.
entry_priceEntry price as specified when opening.
sharesShare count. Optional — null if not provided.
entry_dateTimestamp when the trade was opened.
notesOptional thesis or reason stored with the trade.
is_opentrue while the position is active.
exit_priceExit price recorded when closed. Null while open.
exit_dateTimestamp when the trade was closed. Null while open.
